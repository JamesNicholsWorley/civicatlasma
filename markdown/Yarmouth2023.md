# Yarmouth election: With only one race, Forest reelected to a third term on Select Board

[Rasheek Tabassum Mujib](\staff\7609908001\rasheek-tabassum-mujib) Cape Cod Times May 17, 2023, 12:23 p.m. ET 0:00 / -- Keep Watching

<!-- image -->

[YARMOUTH](https://www.yarmouth.ma.us/) - In the only contested race on the [annual town election](\story\news\2023\05\13\yarmouth-election-may-16-ballot-includes-one-race-for-select-board\70202238007) ballot, Mark Forest was reelected to the Select Board for a fourth term.

Mark Forest and Joe Glynn ran for one [Select Board](https://www.yarmouth.ma.us/65/Selectmen) seat for a three-year term in Tuesday's [election](https://www.yarmouth.ma.us/577/Elections) .

Forest secured the seat with 1,028 votes; Glynn polled 159 votes.

Glynn was more successful in his bid for reelection to [Dennis-Yarmouth Regional School Committee](https://www.dy-regional.k12.ma.us/district/school-committee) . He returned to the board with 575 votes.

Current school board member Phillip Morris, who was not on the ballot, ran a write-in campaign for the second three-year term. According to an email from the town clerk's office he gathered more write-ins than other candidates.

Residents head to the patriotic voting booths at the Yarmouth Senior Center in South Yarmouth on Tuesday to cast their ballots in the town's annual election. Mark Forest was reelected to the Select Board, Joe B. Glynn returned to the Dennis-Yarmouth Regional School Committee along with write-in candidate Phillip Morris.

<!-- image -->

Running unopposed, Moderator Kenneth Mudie was reelected with 1,009 votes; Bambi Rosario-Wyatt was reelected for a five-year term on the Yarmouth Housing Authority with 961 votes and Andrew Hoagland Krauss was elected for a two-year term with 966 votes. Krauss ran to fill a vacancy created when [John Reed passed away earlier this year](\story\news\local\2023\05\01\teacher-advocate-mentor-john-reeds-legacy-cape-cod-remembered\70101235007) .

Among a total of 21,869 eligible voters in the town of Yarmouth with a total of eight precincts, only 1,205 voters cast ballots in Tuesday's election. The voter turnout this year was 5.51%.
