NORTH ADAMS - Incumbents reigned in Tuesday's municipal election, with Mayor Jennifer Macksey back for a second term after a convincing victory, and six [city councilors](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/news/northern_berkshires/north-adams-city-council-candidates-outline-priorities-judge-council-recent-performance-in-response-to-eagle-questionnaire-election/article_df5f2eaa-780c-11ee-97df-07606b62457c.html) reelected.

Macksey won with 1,728 votes to Aprilyn Carsno's 163, according to unofficial results posted by the city clerk almost immediately after polls closed. Leading vote-getters among city councilors included Andrew Fitch, 1,419; Keith Bona, 1,399; Bryan Sapienza, 1,384; and Lisa Blackmer, 1,357.

Macksey said the election shows that "voters have confidence in me, and we look forward to another two years of serving this community to the best of my ability."

"I'm so happy, and I look forward to serving the community for many years to come," Macksey said Tuesday evening on her way into Mingo's for her election party.

##### [Article with images North Adams Mayor Jennifer Macksey is poised for reelection, but challenger Aprilyn Carsno isn't letting her coast to a second term](\news\northern_berkshires\north-adams-mayor-jennifer-macksey-poised-for-reelection-but-challenger-aprilyn-carsno-isnt-letting-the-incumbent-coast-to-a-second-term\article_8adcf9f4-7747-11ee-a2c0-23035f467a49.html)

Macksey ran on her [mayoral record](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/news/northern_berkshires/north-adams-mayor-jennifer-macksey-poised-for-reelection-but-challenger-aprilyn-carsno-isnt-letting-the-incumbent-coast-to-a-second-term/article_8adcf9f4-7747-11ee-a2c0-23035f467a49.html) , as well as her record in the public sector, which is extensive. Carsno forced Macksey to respond to several pointed criticisms during the campaign, including about the mayor's handling of the dismissal of former Police Chief Jason Wood, but it didn't much affect the final outcome. Macksey's promises to the city have revolved around economic development and public safety.

Council newcomers, Fitch, Peter Breen (1,335 votes) and Deanna Morrow (881 votes), will join an experienced body, though it's not without its detractors.

Both Macksey and Carsno called out the council for delaying decisions, and either slowing down or throwing out ideas, in recent years. Still, victorious candidates pointed to the election results as well as choices in the last two years that cautiously push development in the city forward. A [pervasive combativeness](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/news/northern_berkshires/north-adams-council-climate/article_6d1cc2d6-48aa-11ed-b614-3bdb5ab71b62.html) , and petty back-and-forths, had councilors discussing the body's level of toxicity with The Eagle last year. That talk has quieted since.

##### [Article with images North Adams City Council candidates outline priorities and judge council's recent performance](\news\northern_berkshires\north-adams-city-council-candidates-outline-priorities-judge-council-recent-performance-in-response-to-eagle-questionnaire-election\article_df5f2eaa-780c-11ee-97df-07606b62457c.html)

Other incumbent victors Peter Oleskiewicz, Ashley Shade and Wayne Wilkinson earned 1,026, 1,237 and 1,166 votes, respectively. The odd candidates out, Colin Bain and Robert Cardimino, earned 754 votes and 566 votes, respectively.

Though only 1,907 people voted in the mayoral election, or barely 20 percent of the electorate, the talk from winners on Tuesday was that the election was a message to press on the same path.

"I think the election says the current council is going in the right direction," Shade said Tuesday night. "We know that there's a lot of work to do, and we know that there's a lot in process. It says to the people who are new, that the people on the council are trusted."

Peter Breen is looking forward to pushing his pet issues during his first term on the council.

"I want to thank everyone for voting," Breen said. "I'm passionate about doing the infrastructure with the Hoosic River. Having the City Council supporting it is a great thing, it's going to be a lot of good jobs. The other thing is, education is important to me. There may be a new school; I'm excited about that."

For the North Adams School Committee, incumbents Tara Jacobs and Alyssa Tomkowicz were reelected to a four-year term with 1,510 and 1,358 votes, respectively, while newcomer Cody Chamberlain joined them, beating out Eric Wilson 788 votes to 509 votes.

##### [Article with images North Adams and Pittsfield city clerks hope voters beat low turnout predictions for Election Day](\news\local\pittsfield-north-adams-city-election-2023-voter-turnout\article_c25e3a2a-7cd6-11ee-9203-ab13571b13a5.html)

City Clerk Tina Leonesio's prediction on Monday for a relatively low [voter turnout](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/news/local/pittsfield-north-adams-city-election-2023-voter-turnout/article_c25e3a2a-7cd6-11ee-9203-ab13571b13a5.html) came true. With an incumbent mayor in Macksey and a long-shot challenger in Carsno, the mayor's race didn't draw much attention. That, along with no state or federal races on the ballot, could have caused depressed turnout in the city.
