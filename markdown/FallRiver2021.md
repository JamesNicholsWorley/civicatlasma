# Here's who Fall River voters picked for City Council and School Committee

FALL RIVER - There will be three new faces on the School Committee next year, and one new face on the City Council - along with a returning member.

Coogan re-elected: [Incumbent handily wins Fall River mayoral race over challenger Cliff Ponte](\story\news\2021\11\02\fall-river-voters-elect-next-mayor\6256290001)

According to unofficial results, voters on Tuesday returned to office incumbent city councilors Linda Pereira, Trott Lee, Shawn Cadime, Bradford Kilby, Leo Pelletier and Michelle Dionne. Joining their ranks will be Andrew Raposo and former councilor Joseph Camara.

Pereira was the top vote-getter with 5,704 votes, with Lee just behind her at 5,542. Dionne was the ninth-place vote-getter with 3,867.

Councilor Christopher Peckham missed the cutoff in 10th place, with 3,667 votes.

In the city council race, 18 candidates vied for nine empty seats.

The ballot included eight current members of the council - and nine potential newcomers - Camara and Raposo, and Gabriel Amaral, Paulo Amaral, Angel Pantoja Jr., Alexander Silva, Matthew Springer, Ricky Tith and Laura Washington.

## Three new members of School Committee

Shelli Pereira, daughter of City Councilor Linda Pereira, will be joining the School Committee, along with Sara Rodrigues and Bobby Bailey. They came in fourth, fifth and sixth places.

Voters also re-elected incumbents Kevin Aguiar, Paul Hart and Mimi Larrivee.

Aguiar was the top vote-getter with 6,313 votes, above Hart with 5,354. Bailey's 3,922 votes were enough to earn sixth place.

Ten candidates competed for six seats on the board. Among the other challengers were Charles Chase Jr., Melissa Costa Doyle, Collin Dias and John Kaleski.

The unofficial results are as follows (winners are in bold):

There were also 53 write-in votes.

## Voters' voices

Joe Camara, a former 12-term city councilor who sought reelection on Tuesday, said he hoped to bring his 24 years of experience back to the council.

"We have to get back to providing good basic city services without breaking the bank," he said.

Paul Hart outside Tansey Elementary School on election night 2021.

<!-- image -->

Paul Hart, a three-term School Committee member and former city councilor, said he was hoping to be reelected to continue work combating bullying and harassment in the district and addressing chronic absenteeism. With former superintendent Matthew Malone now out of the district and the pandemic fading, he said he's hoping the committee can settle back into their normal work.

"The past two years have been tough," he said.

Bobby Bailey said his first step if elected to the School Committee would be to assess what students and families need most. He also wants to focus on making local schools more strongly connected to the rest of the community.

"It's been a long season with COVID," he said.

Ana Ferreira said she voted to reelect Linda Pereira to the City Council, saying she trusts her to repair the city's aging streets, and for Collin Dias and Sara Rodrigues as new School Committee members who would increase transparency and communication between the schools and parents.

"She's very involved and very forthcoming," she said of Rodrigues.

Sarah Lapointe holds up campaign signs outside Tansey Elementary School with her pup, Jaxon on Tuesday afternoon.

<!-- image -->

Nicole Phenix said Bobby Bailey was her favorite candidate for School Committee, explaining that the district needs someone who could highlight the good things happening in local schools.

"He's a good face for the school system. He's a product of the schools and a good face for what they can do," she said.

Corey Sleep said he voted for Coogan in the hopes of maintaining continuity in the city government.

"You can't just have someone for two years and then get rid of them. It takes 10 years to really change things," he said.
