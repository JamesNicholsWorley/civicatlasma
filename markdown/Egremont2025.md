## **Town Clerk Hours: Monday - Friday, 1:00 PM to 3:00 PM or by Appointment**

## 

## Requests for Public Records

Under Massachusetts 950 CMR 32, all requests for public records must be given to Juliette Haas, Public Records Access Officer, or Mary Brazie, Secondary Public Records Access Officer.

The Public Records Access Officer (RAO) will coordinate responses. Requests for public records may be made orally, including telephone requests, or in writing to the Public Records Access Officer. Written requests may be delivered by hand, mail, email, or fax.

**Mailing Address:** Att: RAO, P.O. Box 368, South Egremont, MA 01258

## Town Election

Egremont's General Bylaws established the second Tuesday in May for the Town Election.

## **Check Your Voter Registration/How to Register**

Active Egremont registered voters do not need to re-register. You are a registered voter if you receive the vote-by-mail postcard in January. You remain active by returning your town street list/annual town census form. The annual town census form will be mailed in mid-January.

You can check your voter registration status or register to vote by visiting the [**Secretary of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts Online Voter Registration System.**](https://www.sec.state.ma.us/ovr/) You can also register to vote by filling out and submitting a paper voter registration application available at Town Hall. The deadline to register to vote is ten days before the election. No matter your method of registering, all new voters will receive a voter confirmation letter in the mail.
