Minutes of the Annual Town Election
April L,2024
The Annual Town Election was held on Monday, April L,2024. Polling hours were from 7:00
a.m. through 8:00 p.m. The Warrant was properly posted.
The Town did not have the option for Early Voting or Vote by Mail for this election, however,
Absentee Ballot applications were accepted until noon the Friday before. The last day to
register to vote for this Election was Friday, March 22nd.
The polls were declared open at 7:00 a.m. by Town Clerk, Cynthia Thompson. There were zero
voters waiting when the polls opened. The Specimen Ballot, lnstructioirs to Voters, Voters Bill of
Rights, and Zero Tabulations from Precinct L, Precinct 2, Precinct 3 and Precinct 4 were posted.
The AutoMark handicap voting machine was turned on.

Morris, Sergeant Steve Eaton and Officer Edward McDonald.
1

The ballot boxes were opened by Sergeant Morris and Town Clerk, Cynthia Thompson. The
ballot boxes proved to be empty and the zero tabulation tapes were run and posted. The keys
were handed over to Sergeant Morris Sergeant Eaton.
The turnout was slow and steady throughout the day. The tabulator in Precinct 4 was testy and
was not allowing the ballots to drop into the ballot box. The tabulator had to be cleared many
times from jamming ballots. A service call will be made to the vendor.
At the time of this AnnualTown Election, there were a totalof 9,821. registered voters in
Wrentham. There were 25 requests for Absentee ballots. This election had a total of 1273
voters just making it close to a !3% total turnout.
Absentee Ballots were processed by the Precinct Clerks during the day. Zero residents took
advantage of the Automark machine. There were several affirmations, no provisional ballots
and a few of spoiled ballots.
At 8:00 p.m. Town Clerk Thompson closed the polls. The voting machines were programmed for
tabulation and two sets of tapes were run. Tellers reconciled their books and write-in votes
were recorded. Unofficial results were ready by 8:40 p.m. There were Board of Health and
Housing Authority vacancies that no nomination papers were pulled, so required write-in votes.
All materials were returned to Town Hall by 10:30 p.m. and un-official results were posted on
the Town's website.
The Town Clerk would like to thank allthe staff members who worked at this election to make
the day run smoothly. Special thanks to Assistant Town Clerk, Ann Fisk for helping to manage
another su I election.
