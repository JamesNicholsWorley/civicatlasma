# Norwell election: Town Clerk Patricia Anderson reelected in three-way race

- Anderson defeated challengers Dannielle Shepherd and Donna Snow.

NORWELL − The town clerk will serve an eighth term in office after winning a three-way race for the position at Saturday's annual town election.

Patricia Anderson, who has been in the role since 2008, received 440 votes. Dannielle Shepherd, general manager of Norwell Package and General Store, received 413 votes, and Donna Snow, administrative assistant to the town's water superintendent, received 210 votes.

The town clerk's race was the only contest on the annual town election ballot.

Norwell Town Clerk Patricia Anderson was reelected to an eighth term.

<!-- image -->

Anderson, 72, has lived in Norwell for 62 years and started working at town hall in 1996 as a data collector for the board of assessors. She then was the assistant town clerk for nine years. She is a 2016 graduate of the New England Municipal Clerks Institute and Academy and a certified municipal clerk.

Brian Greenberg ran unopposed for the seat on the select board now held by outgoing member Ellen Allen. She did not seek reelection to the board after 12 years in office.
