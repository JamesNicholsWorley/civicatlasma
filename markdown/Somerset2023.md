# Election results: selectman unseated, newcomer joining school committee in Somerset.

SOMERSET - A sitting selectmen was unseated and a newcomer will join the school committee in Somerset after Monday's annual town election.

Somerset and Swansea both held [local elections on Monday](\story\news\politics\elections\2023\04\07\local-elections-are-next-week-in-somerset-swansea-and-westport\70087804007) .

In Somerset, current Selectmen Lorne Lawless, who has served multiple stints on the board, ran for one open seat against former school committee and planning board member Jamison Souza and Paul Healey, who currently serves on the Council on Aging Board.

Souza bested Lawless and Healey, securing 1,344 votes out of a total of 2,809 ballots cast in that race, compared to Lawless' 1093 and Healey's 338.

Souza had run largely on bringing smart finances and economic development to the town to help it continue to recover from the loss of tax revenue from the Brayton Point Power Station, [a theme echoed](\story\news\politics\elections\2023\04\07\local-elections-are-next-week-in-somerset-swansea-and-westport\70087804007) by the other two men in the race. He also called for a sense of unity and camaraderie in town politics.

"I wanna bring back a community that is united," he said in a statement for Somerset Public Access TV. "This election is not about any individual."

The town's school committee also had a contested race. Incumbent Robert Gaw was reelected, winning 1,621 votes out of 5,618 total votes cast in the school committee race. Jennifer Ashley, a long-time public schools educator and former employee in both the Somerset and Somerset Berkley Regional School districts, will also join the committee; she garnered 1,625 votes. James Nasto also ran for on of the two open school committee seats and won 1,013 votes.

In Somerset's other two contested races, incumbent Town Moderator Lucia Casey was reelected, while Robert Lima was elected to the water and sewer commission.

## Swansea School Committee Election

In Swansea, incumbents Sonya Barbosa and Thomas Rodriguez, Jr. was both reelected to the school committee in a close race. Barbosa won 464 votes out of 1,662 ballots cast, while Rodriguez scored 460 votes. Frank Baptista, Jr, who also ran, secured 424 votes according to [unofficial election results](https://cms8.revize.com/revize/swanseama/Unofficial%20Results%2004-10-223.pdf) .

The race for school committee was Swansea's only contested election.
