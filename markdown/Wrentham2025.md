Minutes of the Annual Town Election
April 7,2025
The Annual Town Election was held on Monday, April 7, 2025. Polling hours were from 7:00
a.m. through 8:00 p.m. The Warrant was properly posted.
The Town did not have the option for Early Voting or Vote by Mail for this election, however
Absentee Ballot applications were accepted until noon on Thursday, April 3rd. The last day to
register to vote for this Election was Friday, March 28th.
The ballot boxes were opened by Sergeant Morris and Town Clerk, Cynthia Thompson. The
ballot boxes proved to be empty and the Zero Tabulation tapes were run. The keys were
handed over to Sergeant Morris and then to Sergeant Eaton.
The Specimen Ballot, lnstructions to Voters, Voters Bill of Rights, and Zero Tabulation tapes
from Precinct L, Precinct 2, Precinct 3, and Precinct 4 were posted. The AutoMark handicap
voting machine was turned on and tested.
The polls were declared open at 7:00 a.m. by Town Clerk, Cynthia Thompson. There were five
(5) voters waiting when the polls opened.

Many special thanks to the Wrentham Elementary School custodial staff and the Town's DPW
and Facilities department. Thank you also to the Wrentham Police Department for working the
detail (Sergeant Dan Morris, Sergeant Steve Eaton, and the Special Police Officers working the
traffic details).
It was a rainy and raw dismal day, but the turnout was steady
There were no candidates on the Ballot running for the following positions: the Board of
Assessors, the Wrentham Housing Authority, and the Planning Board (two vacancies).
Write-in campaigns were planned by several residents which caused significant confusion
among the voters as to how to write the name and address of the candidate of choice in the
very limited space along with completion of the oval. This also contributed to the number of
residents that over-voted and ultimately ended in over 115 spoiled ballots at the close of polls.
This is the greatest number of spoiled ballots that I have ever encountered in any election in my
ten years as Clerk.
At the time of this Annual Town Election, there was a total of 9,937 registered voters in
Wrentham. There were 45 requests for Absentee ballots. This election had a total of t,927
voters, making a L9% total turnout, which has not happened in a very long time for any local
Wrentham election.
Absentee Ballots were processed by the Precinct Clerks during the day. Zero residents took
advantage of the Automark machine. There were a few affirmations and no provisional ballots
were cast.
At 8:00 p.m., Town Clerk Thompson closed the polls. The voting machines were programmed
for tabulation and two sets of tapes were run. Tellers reconciled their books and write-in votes
were recorded. Unofficial results were ready by 9:00 p.m. There were Board of Health and
Housing Authority vacancies that no nomination papers were pulled, so required write-in votes.
All materials were returned to Town Hall after midnight.
The Town Clerk would like to thank all the staff members who worked at this election to make
the day run smoothly. Congratulations to all the candidates, especially Ann Fisk, the next Town
Clerk for Wrentham and welcome to the new Assistant Town Clerk, Michele Doll for helping to
manage
