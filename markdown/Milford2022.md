# Results are in. Milford annual election determines three contested races

Portrait of Toni Caushi

<!-- image -->

[Toni Caushi](https://www.telegram.com/staff/8323186002/toni-caushi/)

The Milford Daily News

Updated April 6, 2022, 7:49 a.m. ET Keep Watching

MILFORD - Incumbent John Erickson will be joined by newcomer Robyn Bratica on the School Committee as they edged out two others in the most heavily contested race in the town's annual election on Tuesday.

Bratica topped the ticket with 973 votes, followed by Erickson with 739. Derrick Sobers (640) and R.J. Sheedy (541), the latter of whom was appointed to the board to fill a vacancy last summer, came up short in their bids.

**RELATED** : [Sheedy appointed to School Committee](\story\news\2021\08\25\rj-sheedy-appointed-milford-school-committee\5590651001)

In a statement, Erickson said his third term come with a commitment toward the "renovation or replacement project at Milford High School."

Bratica, a 2002 Milford High graduate, holds a doctorate in psychology.

With just three contested races, turnout was lighter than usual this spring - 1,742 (9.6%) of Milford's 18,209 registered voters came to the polls, according to unofficial results from the Town Clerk's Office. Twelve percent came out a year ago.

Candidates and supporters hold signs in an effort to attract votes outside the Milford Portuguese Club, one of three polling locations in town, April 5, 2022.

<!-- image -->

In other contested races, Jose Morais topped John DePaolo Jr. for a five-year term on the Planning Board, 833-776, and Scott Crisafulli defeated Charles Reneau for park commissioner, 936-633.

Morais has a 40-year background in quality assurance and engineering, according to his Facebook campaign page.

"We need careful, responsible planning that is best conceived through someone who has the technical experience, will listen to the needs of our residents and work collaboratively with the community and other town boards," he said in a statement ahead of the election.

Milford voter Suzie Batista tends to her grandson, 6-month-old Logan Joseph Therrien, while marking her ballot at the Milford Portuguese Club polling station, April 5, 2022. Less than 10% of the town's registered voters participated in the annual election.

<!-- image -->

For Parks Commission, Crisafulli will bring 32 years of experience in the Milford Highway Department, including his most current position of highway surveyor.

In uncontested races, incumbent and Michael Walsh earned 1,282 votes to keep his seat on the Select Board for another three years; Paul Mazzuchelli returns for a three-year term on the Board of Health after collectiong 1,420 votes; Paul Simas took over a three-year term on the Board of Assessors with 1,117 votes; and Steven Borges won a one-year unexpired term with 1,174 votes.

Voters in Milford's Precinct 8 check in at the Portuguese Club polling station, April 5, 2022.

<!-- image -->

For the Board of Library Trustees, the two available three-year seats went to incumbent Stephanie Abisla (1,031 votes) and newcomer Sarah Moazeni (899 votes).

For Sewer Commission, incumbent Richard Cenedella returns after earning 1,196 votes, while Leonardo Morcone Jr. won a one-year unexpired term with 1,288 votes.

Incumbent Kristen Gardner and Frederick Elliott won three-year seats as Trustees of Vernon Grove Cemetery with 1,001 and 806 votes, respectively.

Milford voters mark their ballots at the Milford Portuguese Club polling station during the town's annual town election, April 5, 2022. The election contained just three contested races.

For Constable, incumbents Mark Calzolaio (999 votes), Raymond Pagucci Jr. (977 votes), Nadine Ladeau (901 votes), and newcomer Robert Sheedy (833 votes) took four of the five available three-year seats,

For Town Meeting representative, each of the town's eight precincts had fewer candidates than the 10 available seats, thus giving all candidates on the ballot three-year terms. Winners were as follows:

- Precinct 1. Incumbents Suzan Clark (81), Nicole C. M. Fulginiti (81), Rosanna Blanchard Erickson (74) and Maria Ozella (66)
- Precinct 2. Incumbents Gianna Krovocheck (147), William Sanborn III (124), Michael Aghajanian (116), Thomas Russ (116) and William Wing (116)
- Precinct 3. Incumbents John Taddei (113), Julie Gonzalez (110), Rosemary Trettel (98), Alfred Teixeira (95), Bruce Meacham Jr. (94), Russell Abisla (93), Stephanie Abisla (92), and B. Gregory Johnson (92)
- Precinct 4. Incumbents Evelyn D. Bon Tempo (84), Martha White (78) and Terence Thomas (73)
- Precinct 5. Newcomer Alexis A. C. Forgit (179) and incumbents Jose Morais (213), Michael Detore (187), R.J. Sheedy (175), Joanne Dillon (172), David Hunter (162) and Edwin Roth (156)
- Precinct 6. Incumbents Angelo Calagione (170), Jennifer Parson (161), Peter Pacella (160), Christine Crean (149), Andrea Ellsworth (146), Daniel Cloutier (146), John Crean Jr. (145), Matthew Delaney (145) and Brendan Rickert (141)
- Precinct 7. Incumbents Paul Tamagni (138), Mark Nelson (136), Renaldo DeLuzio (135), Nancy Wojick (134), Meghan Hornberger (130), Beverly Swymer (123) and George Swymer Jr. (120),
- Precinct 8. Incumbents James Griffith (170), Stephen Costello (168), Robert Benson (158) and Robert Delmore (157)

Results will become official by the end of week, according to the Town Clerk's office.
