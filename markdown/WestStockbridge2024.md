West Stockbridge Select Board member Andrew Krouss won a second term, gaining support from 275 voters at Monday's annual town election. Challenger Wayne Cooper came in second, scoring 213 votes. Krouss said he was relieved and very happy, terming the campaign civil and free of animosity. CLARENCE FANTO - THE BERKSHIRE EAGLE

<!-- image -->

WEST STOCKBRIDGE - In a high-turnout town election with 43 percent of registered voters casting ballots, [Selectman Andrew Krouss](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/news/central_berkshires/west-stockbridge-annual-election-includes-a-contest-for-select-board-and-the-departure-of-a-veteran-town-moderator/article_254ba19c-0722-11ef-9ec4-0f260ad31b5b.html) won a second three-year term.

Krouss scored 275 votes, while challenger Wayne Cooper, who runs the town's waste disposal facility, got 213 votes.

"I'm relieved; I'm really happy," Krouss said. "It was a tight race but clean, very well-disciplined, we didn't have any contentious animosity. We're ready with boots on the ground to get back to business [at the Select Board]."

Krouss cited several top priorities - including [the proposed downtown dog park](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/news/central_berkshires/west-stockbrdige-town-meeting-results-2023/article_b58dc272-0c99-11ef-ac00-e3a89bab16f4.html) and the [Wiseacre Farm marijuana odor-control plan](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/news/central_berkshires/west-stockbridge-leaders-hire-tech-company-to-monitor-wiseacre-farms-marijuana-odor-control-strategy/article_3519d276-d1b0-11ee-a118-7b30b299e4ed.html) . Also on the Select Board's agenda is discussing potential options for shared fire and ambulance services.

"We're trying to keep a calm lid on everything," he said, "and if we do, everyone will be happy." As for concerns about the property tax burden, Krouss pointed out that the top expense is the annual budget payment to the [Berkshire Hills Regional School District](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/news/southern_berkshires/great-barrington-monument-mountain-high-school-berkshire-hills-renovate-rebuild-ozawa-hall/article_347091f2-dc05-11ee-ab56-f743f85633c7.html) . "We have no control over it," he noted.

"The people spoke," said Cooper, as he was congratulated by several supporters for "a good try."

"I'm not disappointed," he added. "People asked me to run, and I did." He noted that at his transfer station post, he hears complaints from residents "about what's going wrong with the town."

Wayne Cooper came in second with 213 votes in his race to unseat Select Board member Andrew Krouss, who won a second term with 275 votes. "The people spoke," said Cooper, adding that he wasn't disappointed. CLARENCE FANTO -- THE BERKSHIRE EAGLE

<!-- image -->

The main issue is taxes, he said, claiming that "one week's take-home pay a month goes to real estate taxes. That tells you something's wrong."

All other openings were uncontested. Joseph Roy Jr., was the top vote-getter for town moderator, with 439 in favor. The former owner of the Floor Store succeeds [Eugene Dellea, who stepped down](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/opinion/editorials/our-opinion-3-cheers-for-a-longtime-moderator-in-west-stockbridge/article_258d0380-0d62-11ef-a0ef-63f9bf83fca0.html) after 58 years in the post, a state record for moderator.

Also endorsed by voters on Monday were Frank Landsberger and Mark Greenlaw for two three-year seats on the Finance Committee; Susan Lupo for Board of Assessors; Jared Gelormino and Andrew Fudge for two Planning Board slots; Kathryn Korte for Board of Health; Beth Sack for Library Trustee; and Earl Moffatt for Cemetery Commission.

A ballot question setting the stage for a future town meeting approval of a property tax break for eligible seniors won with 397 ballots in favor, and 36 opposed.

Out of 1,139 registered voters, 491 turned out at Town Hall for the annual election.
